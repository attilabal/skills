# Quality Gates

Use these gates before finalizing complex, external-facing, executive-facing, decision-facing, or source-sensitive work.

## Gate 1 - Source Discipline

Check:

- Which claims come directly from provided input?
- Which claims come from verified external sources?
- Which claims are assumptions, hypotheses, or inferences?
- Are any document-, project-, or account-specific facts unsupported?

Action:

- remove unsupported factual claims;
- qualify inferences;
- label assumptions;
- request missing input or proceed with constraints when necessary.

## Gate 2 - Source Gaps

Ask:

```text
Could this missing information materially change the main answer or safe use of the output?
```

If yes, use stop/go/source-expansion logic. If no, do not overburden the output with peripheral caveats.

## Gate 3 - Contradictions

Check whether inputs conflict on:

- dates;
- owners;
- commitments;
- scope;
- status;
- pricing or commercial terms;
- risk severity;
- stakeholder positions.

Surface material contradictions clearly. Do not silently average conflicting sources.

## Gate 4 - Overclaiming

Look for claims that sound stronger than the evidence supports.

Common offenders:

- "the client wants" when the input only implies interest;
- "the project will slip" when the evidence only suggests risk;
- "we can commit" when the delivery basis is not verified;
- "no legal issue" when no legal review occurred.

Replace with calibrated language.

## Gate 5 - Audience Fit

Check whether the output fits its audience:

- internal scratch work can be rougher;
- team drafts can include working assumptions;
- executive material should be concise and decision-ready;
- client material should avoid unsupported claims and internal uncertainty leakage;
- RFP/contract-related material should avoid accidental commitments.

## Gate 6 - Usability

The final output should help the next action. Ask:

- Can the user send, decide, brief, compare, or assign from this?
- Is the structure chosen for the task rather than copied from a template?
- Are open questions separated from conclusions?
- Are risks specific enough to act on?
- Are constraints visible without overwhelming the answer?

## Gate 7 - Worker Reliance

If worker output was used, verify:

- the delegation card was clear;
- the verification level matched the risk;
- the lead agent accepted, revised, or rejected the output explicitly;
- final claims do not hide weak worker assumptions.

Worker output is candidate input. The lead agent owns final synthesis.
